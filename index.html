Top Attractions in Galveston

Moody Gardens

Explore three glass pyramids housing an aquarium, rainforest, and discovery museum. Perfect for families!

Galveston Island Historic Pleasure Pier

Enjoy thrilling rides, games, and food venues over the Gulf. Try the Iron Shark rollercoaster!

The Strand Historic District

Stroll through Victorian-era buildings with shops, galleries, and eateries.

The Bryan Museum

Dive into Texas history with 70,000 artifacts in a restored orphanage.

Restaurants & Cafes

Gaido’s Seafood Restaurant

A Galveston tradition since 1911, serving fresh Gulf seafood.

Porch Cafe

Coastal cuisine in a relaxed setting in the Beachtown community.

Hearsay on the Strand

Historic ambiance with modern cuisine in the James Fadden Building.

Shrimp ’N Stuff

Try the iconic besos (shrimp-stuffed jalapeños) at this local favorite.

Weather in Galveston

Galveston has a humid subtropical climate with mild winters (January avg: 46.2°F) and hot, humid summers (July avg: 85.9°F). The best times to visit are March–April and October–November for pleasant weather and lower crowds. Check the National Weather Service for updates.

Cruises from Galveston

Galveston is the fourth busiest cruise port in the U.S., offering year-round sailings to Mexico, the Caribbean, and beyond with Carnival, Royal Caribbean, Disney, Norwegian, and Princess. Explore pre-cruise attractions like the Pleasure Pier or The Bryan Museum.
